Project Overview

FINDING A VOICE

Balance(!!) was at the center of this entire project. Admittedly, I found it rather difficult in the beginning to squeeze my own voice into the project. As I made myself more aware of Sato's visual intentions, beyond face value, it became easier to adopt a similar decision-making process; doing my best to embody his values within my own thinking.

Sato once spoke of a tea ceremony being much more than the physical act of coming together and drinking, but is shaped by the cultural richness that grounds it. In the same way, his work expresses auras that are beyond therealm of physicality, exploring realities that may be beyond our current comprehension.

Poster

ARTIFACT 1

I found myself exploring many iterations to this poster before settling on the final. Settled into the Japanese letterforms are layered Images of Sato's 'Box Studies', they're difficult to pick up, but through experimenting with blending modes and hue adjustments, they add subtle gradation to the letter's curves. The airbrushed path that slices through the letterform pays homage to a recurring visual motif in Sato's later works.
